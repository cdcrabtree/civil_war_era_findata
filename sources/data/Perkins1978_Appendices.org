Explanatory notes for Appendix 1:

1. In the Davis-Hughes column, the interest rate series used to calculate exchange rates were
   as follows: 1835 to 1839-Bigelow's 60- to 90-day rate; 1840 to 1860-Davis' textile series,

   Trotter's bill prices (table A-1 in the Davis-Hughes article) using the quarterly mean of the
   Bank of England's rediscount rate. Beginning in 1870 the figures are mean averages of daily
   selling rates listed in the Financial Review, for 3-day bills from 1870 to 1879 and sight bills
   from 1880 to 1900. My research assistant, Steve Mutnick, performed this tedious task, and I
   valued his assistance very highly.
